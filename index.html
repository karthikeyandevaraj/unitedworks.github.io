What is EPR ?

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is a policy approach that places responsibility for the end-of-life management of a product on the manufacturer or producer, rather than the consumer or the government. EPR is based on the principle that those who design, produce, and sell products should also be responsible for managing the waste generated by those products.

Under an EPR system, producers are required to take responsibility for the environmental impact of their products throughout their entire life cycle, including disposal and recycling. This can include designing products for easy disassembly and recycling, setting up collection and recycling systems, and financing the costs of managing and recycling their products.

EPR policies are aimed at reducing the environmental impact of products and promoting the development of sustainable production and consumption practices. EPR is used in a number of industries, including electronics, packaging, and automobiles, and has been implemented in many countries around the world.

E-waste Management for MNCs

At UnitedWorks, we understand the devastating impact that improper handling of electronic waste can have on our environment and our health. The fact that only 8% of the 2 million tons of E-waste disposed of in India is handled by registered recyclers is alarming, as it means that a large portion of this waste is ending up in the hands of immature recyclers who use unorthodox and dangerous methods to extract valuable materials.

The use of cyanide and sulfuric acid to extract gold from E-waste is just one example of the harmful practices being used by these unregistered recyclers. The waste generated from this process is often disposed of in nearby streams, ponds, and landfills, causing significant environmental damage and putting our health at risk.

The cost of this environmental damage is staggering, with the country incurring 10 billion rupees per annum in damages and an additional 5 billion rupees being spent on medical bills. At UnitedWorks, we believe that it is essential to work with certified registered recyclers who have a reputation in the E-waste industry and the sound technology needed to properly dispose of this waste.

By partnering with these reputable recyclers, we can help to ensure that E-waste is handled responsibly, protecting our environment and our health. At UnitedWorks, we are committed to promoting sustainable practices and reducing the impact of E-waste on our planet. Join us in our mission to create a cleaner, healthier future for all

Buy & Sell Scrap Platform

In today's world, waste management is more important than ever. As the population grows and consumption increases, the amount of waste produced continues to rise. However, the waste ecosystem is often fragmented, with sellers and buyers operating in different spheres. This can make it difficult for businesses and individuals to properly dispose of their waste, and for waste management companies to efficiently collect and process it.

That's where a platform that bridges the gaps in the waste ecosystem comes in. By enabling sellers and buyers of waste to transact on one platform, businesses and individuals can more easily find the waste management solutions they need. This can include everything from recycling and composting services to waste removal and disposal.

Additionally, a platform that brings together the various players in the waste ecosystem can help promote sustainability and reduce waste. It can connect those who generate waste with those who need it, such as companies that use recycled materials in their manufacturing processes. This can create a more circular economy, where waste is seen as a valuable resource rather than a problem to be disposed of.

In conclusion, a software that bridges gaps in the waste ecosystem can have a significant impact on waste management and sustainability. By bringing together sellers and buyers of waste, it can help businesses and individuals find the waste management solutions they need, while also promoting a more circular economy.
